Stardust · console redesign

A calm operations console for a durable agent.

Stardust runs an AI agent on your laptop on a Temporal-native runtime. This redesign has two jobs that pull apart — let an operator get work done, and make the durable machinery visible and trustworthy — and resolves them so neither wins at the other's expense. Designed from first principles on the Cinder design system. Dark, desktop-first, engineer lens on. A static review gallery: nine surfaces, the durability layer, every run state, and responsive frames.

Cinder design system · dark Split working surface · 1a Under-the-hood lens · in place Durability as co-headline

Calm by default, dense on demand

The operator's working surface stays quiet. Depth is always one switch — never a different product — away, and it layers in place.

Safety reads as clarity

Risky actions are never hidden or rushed. The approver sees the exact command, file impact, and edited arguments before anything runs. Secrets show names only.

Durability is a co-headline

Retries, approval waits, and crash recovery are stated proudly in plain language. The lens swaps that for raw Temporal vocabulary to verify.

Live is a view over durable truth

Streaming is a fast preview; SQLite transcript is the truth. Every status claim is drillable, and a refresh re-renders identically.
